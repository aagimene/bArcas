# Kayak design history

An organized survey of kayak hull design — by category, with attention to what each category optimizes for and why.

Each category page covers:

1. **Historical context** — where and when.
2. **Representative designs** — specific named hulls with rough dimensions.
3. **Design priorities** — what variables designers optimize (and trade off).
4. **Typical hydrostatic/hydrodynamic signature** — LWL/B, Cp, GM, drag profile.

## Categories at a glance

| Category | LOA (m) | Beam (m) | Cp typical | L/B | Primary optimization |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Greenland qajaq | 5.0 – 5.5 | 0.45 – 0.55 | 0.55 | 9–11 | Rolling, low wind profile, hunting stealth |
| Sea kayak (classic) | 4.7 – 5.5 | 0.52 – 0.62 | 0.55–0.60 | 8–10 | Tracking, capacity, seaworthiness |
| Expedition kayak | 5.3 – 5.8 | 0.56 – 0.62 | 0.57–0.62 | 8–9 | Cargo capacity, tracking |
| Mariner Coaster | 3.81 | 0.66 | ~0.57 | ~6 | Play, surf, maneuver; short LWL is intentional |
| Recreational | 3.0 – 4.0 | 0.65 – 0.80 | ~0.60 | 4–5 | Initial stability |
| Surfski | 5.2 – 6.5 | 0.41 – 0.50 | 0.62–0.66 | 11–14 | Speed in open ocean, downwind surf |
| K1 sprint | 5.20 (max) | 0.30 – 0.40 | 0.60–0.66 | 13–17 | Flat-water speed |
| Whitewater playboat | 1.8 – 2.4 | 0.60 – 0.70 | — (planing) | 3–4 | Maneuverability, planing |
| Whitewater creeker | 2.5 – 2.8 | 0.62 – 0.70 | ~0.58 | 4 | Buoyancy, resurfacing |

(These are rough ranges; individual models vary. Numbers in specific category pages are more precise.)

## ArcasBoat reference library

Phase 2 ships a **reference library** — parametric reconstructions (not exact copies) of iconic designs, usable as forking starting points:

- "Greenland-style qajaq" (after measurements in Harvey Golden's catalogs).
- "Nordkapp-style" (long, narrow, skeg, expedition).
- "Coaster-style" (short, soft-chined, playful).
- "K1-style" (ICF-legal length, narrow, low rocker).
- "Surfski-style" (long, narrow, rockered, planing-capable).
- "Recreational-style" (short, beamy).
- "Whitewater creeker-style."

These are educational reference points, not legal replicas.
