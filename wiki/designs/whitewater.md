# Whitewater kayaks

Short, highly rockered, purpose-built for moving water. Shares little with sea kayak design other than "paddler sits inside and uses a double-bladed paddle."

---

## Subcategories

- **Playboats** — 1.8–2.3 m. Flat-bottomed, highly rockered, designed for tricks: cartwheels, loops, surfing holes.
- **River runners** — 2.3–2.7 m. Compromise between playing and getting downriver.
- **Creekers** — 2.5–2.9 m. High volume, rounded ends, designed to resurface quickly after drops.
- **Slalom kayaks** — ICF-regulated (typically 3.5 m min length, specific weight). Gate racing on artificial whitewater.
- **Wildwater / downriver** — ICF-regulated. Longer (4.2 m min), narrow, displacement-style.

All share: **rocker**, **short LOA**, **durability** (almost exclusively rotomolded plastic or heavy composite).

---

## Playboats

### Typical
| Variable | Value |
|---|---|
| LOA | 1.8 – 2.3 m |
| Beam | 0.60 – 0.70 m |
| Rocker | 0.30 – 0.50 m (huge) |
| Cockpit | Deep, snug |
| Cross-section | Flat planing surface bottom, hard chines |
| Typical mass | 15–20 kg |

### What they optimize
- **Planing** on wave faces — flat, hard-chined hulls plane like a wakeboard.
- **Low volume ends** — bow and stern can be pushed underwater by the paddler for cartwheels and squirts.
- **Snappy edge control** — hard chines catch and release sharply.
- **Maneuverability** — near-zero directional stability. Turn on a dime.

Drag in a displacement sense is irrelevant — playboats aren't paddled for distance.

---

## Creekers

### Typical
| Variable | Value |
|---|---|
| LOA | 2.5 – 2.9 m |
| Beam | 0.62 – 0.72 m |
| Rocker | 0.20 – 0.35 m |
| Volume | 300–400 L (high) |
| Cross-section | Rounded bottom, soft or no chines |
| Typical mass | 18–25 kg |

### What they optimize
- **Resurfacing** after big drops — high volume pushes the bow back up.
- **Forgiving handling** — rounded bottoms slide predictably.
- **Capacity** for throw bags, rescue gear.
- **Bulletproof construction** — plastic, thick walls.

---

## Slalom kayaks

ICF slalom K1 rules (approximate, confirm rulebook):
- Minimum length: 3.50 m
- Minimum weight: 9 kg
- Narrow, light, hull with minimal rocker.
- Paddler sits low; deck is fine.

Designed to transit gates on whitewater courses quickly. Not really comparable to play/creek boats.

---

## Wildwater / downriver kayaks

Completely different beast. Long (≥ 4.2 m per ICF), narrow, displacement-hulled, designed for straight-line speed through whitewater. Looks more like a K1 than a whitewater boat. Specialized class.

---

## Why we document this even though we might not emphasize it

The user is building a kayak hull design app, and "kayak" is a broad category. Sea/touring/sprint/surfski are the core audiences, but we should at least expose the variables that matter for whitewater design — rocker, planing surfaces, hard chines — so the app is not accidentally restrictive.

Planing-surface analysis (Savitsky) is a specific physics domain; we're not targeting it in phase 1.
