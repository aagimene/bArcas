# Future Directions & Ideas for bArcas

bArcas (formerly ArcasBoat, born from the same origin story) is evolving beyond a simple visualizer into a comprehensive, interactive platform for naval architecture, parametric design, and hydrodynamics education.

## 1. Educational "Explainograms"
Building on the concept of the `michell` prototype, bArcas will host a suite of interactive, 3D presentations ("explainograms") that teach complex naval architecture concepts to laypeople.
- **Parametric Design**: Demystifying how equations dictate hull shape.
- **Wave Resistance**: Visualizing Michell's equations and how ships create waves.
- **Stability and Seakeeping**: Interactive sandboxes showing the effects of center of buoyancy and gravity during roll/pitch.

## 2. The "Full Form" Advanced Editor
While explainograms guide users step-by-step, the "Full Form" editor will be the ultimate sandbox:
- Unlocked parametric 3D editor.
- Real-time drag, stability, and wave resistance computations.
- Environmental variables (water density, viscosity) adjustments.
- Ability to export optimized hull lines for physical modeling.

## 3. Automated Hull Optimization
Using theories like Michell's integral combined with modern computational power, bArcas will eventually feature an "Optimizer". Users will input constraints (e.g., minimum volume, maximum draft, desired speed) and the system will auto-generate the hull form with the least wave resistance.

## 4. Side-by-Side Comparison Arena
Expanding on the Swan vs. Raven historical race, bArcas will allow users to pit two custom designs against each other. By synchronizing environmental variables and speed sliders, users can visually and analytically determine which hull performs better across different regimes.

## 5. Gamification of Design
Introducing gamified elements, such as "Thiccness" limits, "Blush" UI effects for extreme parameters, and challenges (e.g., "Design a hull that beats the Wigley benchmark at Froude number 0.4").
