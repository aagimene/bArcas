# Kayak anatomy

Kayak-specific terminology. General hull terms are in [hull-terms.md](hull-terms.md).

---

## Major parts

- **Hull** — the bottom half of the boat, below the sheer.
- **Deck** — the top half, above the sheer. Sea kayaks are usually fully decked with a cockpit hole; sit-on-tops have a molded deck with drain holes.
- **Sheer** — where hull and deck meet. Usually the widest point.
- **Cockpit** — the opening in the deck where the paddler sits. Sized and shaped for the paddler (keyhole, ocean, recreational).
- **Cockpit coaming** — the raised rim around the cockpit where a spray skirt attaches.
- **Bow** — front.
- **Stern** — back.
- **Keel line** — center-bottom of the hull in profile; its curvature is the **rocker**.
- **Chine** — the transition between hull bottom and hull side.
- **Bilge** — the curved lower portion of the hull side, between chine and flat bottom (if distinguishable).
- **Topsides** — the hull sides above the waterline.

## Cockpit-area features

- **Seat / bucket seat** — molded or padded seat in the cockpit.
- **Seat pan height** — vertical distance from the hull bottom to the top of the seat; affects KG.
- **Footbraces / foot pegs** — adjustable foot rests.
- **Thigh braces** — pads or bracing on the underside of the deck at the cockpit, where paddler thighs apply control force.
- **Knee braces / knee tubes** — similar function; traditional Greenland.
- **Hip pads** — pads at the paddler's hips for bracing.
- **Back band / back rest** — lumbar support.

## Deck features

- **Deck camber / deck crown** — transverse curvature of the deck. More camber = more deck volume and head clearance.
- **Deck peak** — the height of the deck above baseline at a given station.
- **Foredeck** — the deck forward of the cockpit.
- **Afterdeck** — the deck aft of the cockpit.
- **Masik** — the forward deck beam in traditional Greenland construction, typically curved and giving shape to the foredeck.
- **Hatches** — covered openings in the deck for cargo storage.
- **Bulkheads** — transverse walls inside the hull that separate hatch compartments and provide flotation.
- **Deck lines / perimeter lines** — bungee or rope runs on the deck, used for securing gear and for rescue grabs.

## Underwater features

- **Skeg** — a deployable fin at the stern, usually retractable into a skeg box. Improves tracking without active steering.
- **Rudder** — a pivoting fin at the stern, controlled by foot pedals. Active steering + tracking aid.
- **Dagger board / drop keel** — less common on kayaks; a retractable keel fin amidships, found on some sailing kayaks.
- **Skeg box** — internal recess for the retractable skeg.

## Paddler interface

- **Paddle** — the propulsion tool; typically a double-bladed paddle (though Greenland single-blade storm paddles and some traditional types exist).
- **Spray skirt / spray deck** — fabric or neoprene worn by paddler and attached to the coaming to keep water out.

---

## Rolling and bracing (relevant to design)

- **Layback** — lying back on the afterdeck to finish a roll. Requires low afterdeck volume.
- **Greenland-style roll** — low-brace-assisted roll that uses a layback. Favored by traditional qajaq designs.
- **Combat roll / C-to-C** — an upright roll used in whitewater; requires a more neutral deck shape.

Hull/deck shape influences which rolling style the boat supports well.

## Kayak categories (brief — see [../designs/](../designs/) for depth)

- **Sea kayak** — 4.5–5.5 m, decked, touring focus, usually with skeg or rudder, two bulkhead compartments. See [../designs/sea-kayaks.md](../designs/sea-kayaks.md).
- **Touring / expedition kayak** — subset of sea kayaks, longer, higher volume for gear. See [../designs/touring.md](../designs/touring.md).
- **Recreational kayak** — 3–4 m, wider cockpit, more stable, less performance. See [../designs/recreational.md](../designs/recreational.md).
- **Surfski** — sit-on-top racing kayak for ocean, 5.2–6.5 m. See [../designs/surfski.md](../designs/surfski.md).
- **K1 / sprint kayak** — flat-water racer, sit-in, ICF-spec. See [../designs/k1-sprint.md](../designs/k1-sprint.md).
- **Whitewater kayak** — 2–3 m, short, highly rockered, planing or displacement depending on style. See [../designs/whitewater.md](../designs/whitewater.md).
- **Greenland qajaq / baidarka** — traditional Inuit designs. See [../designs/history-of-kayak-design.md](../designs/history-of-kayak-design.md).

## Construction methods (for reference, not for bArcas output)

bArcas targets **CNC-milled plugs, 3D-printed plugs, and composite molds**. Other construction methods exist and are documented here for completeness:

- **Strip-built** — wood strips over station molds. Out of bArcas scope.
- **Stitch-and-glue** — plywood panels stitched and glued. Out of bArcas scope.
- **Skin-on-frame (SOF)** — ribs + stringers + fabric skin; traditional Greenland/Aleutian. Out of bArcas scope.
- **Composite layup** — wet / prepreg / infusion over a mold. **In scope** (we produce the mold).
- **Thermoforming** — heated plastic sheet formed over a mold. **Future in scope**.
- **Rotomolding** — plastic powder rotated in a heated mold. **Future in scope**.
